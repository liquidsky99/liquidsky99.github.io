I feed birds sunflower seeds and peanuts. Some birds hide food to retrieve and eat at a later time. This behavior is called “caching” and it helps birds survive during bad weather and when food sources are low. The blue bird called Steller jay seems to swallow many shelled sunflower seeds. I'm curious if they reguratate them later to store somewhere else. The Golden crowned kinglet, second one from the left, which is really small stays high in trees and comes down just to drink water from the bird bath. The sapsucker which has a red head in the first row, pecks holes in the tree bark and returns to eat insects caught in the sap from the tree. The sapsucker is a type of Woodpeckers. I saw an Scrub jay on the fence only once. I heard that they usually live further south. The brown creeper, third row second column, looks for insects in the tree bark. They always start at the bottom and move up the tree looking for bugs and when they get to the top they fly back to the bottom to start over.
