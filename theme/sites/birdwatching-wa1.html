Nisqually National Wildlife Refuge

The Nisqually National Wildlife Refuge is located between Tacoma and Olympia and encompasses almost the entirety of the Nisqually River Delta, a biologically rich and diverse area. Birds commonly seem here are Bald Eagles, Great Blue Herons, Caspian Turns, Kingfishers, many species of ducks, gulls and swallows, along with many species of perching birds or passerines. On my visit, in addition to the above, I saw Common yellow throats, Cedar waxwings, Savanna sparrows, Marsh wrens, American gold finches and redwing blackbirds. I saw that this Bald eagle was annoyed by two Red wing black birds there.

Discovery Park

Discovery Park is a 534-acre park on the shores of Puget Sound in the Magnolia neighborhood of Seattle, Washington. It is the city's largest public park and contains 11.81 miles of walking trails. Many species of birds can be found in Discovery Park. On my visit I saw American gold finches, Cedar wax wings, Red tailed hawks, Downy woodpeckers, Song sparrows, Chickadees, and House finches among other birds.

Olympic Park

Olympic National Park is an American national park located in the State of Washington, on the Olympic Peninsula. On my birding trip I spent time both inside and outside the park. Many species of birds can be found in and around Olympic National Park. I saw Bald Eagles, turkey vultures, Gulls, Kingfishers, Great Blue Herons, Western king bird, Downy woodpeckers, Song sparrows, chickadees, Common mergansers and Yellow warblers among other birds.
