Mercer Slough Nature Park

The Mercer Slough Nature Park is a 320-acre wetland just south of Bellevue populated with ferns, shrubs, flowers, several varieties of berries, distinctive wooden boardwalks and shaded pathways. Birds commonly found here include several species of ducks including this beautiful wood ducks. I've also seen Yellow rumpted warblers, Marsh wrens, Downy woodpeckers, Coopers hawks, Oregon juncos, Chickadees, and Robins.

Marymore Park

Marymoor Park, located on the north end of Lake Sammamish in Redmond, Washington, is King County's largest, oldest, and most popular park, that covers 640 acres. On my visit to Marymoor park we saw Common mergansers, Red tailed hawks, Osprey, Purple Martins, Coots, western Flycatchers, Black headed grosbeaks among other birds. Purple martins in this photo look like Swallows had manmade nests in the lake.

Union Bay

The Union Bay Natural Area in Seattle, Washington, also known as Union Bay Marsh, is the restored remainder of the filled former Union Bay and Union Bay Marsh. On our visits to Union Bay I saw Buffle headed ducks, Northern shoveler and Wood ducks, Brant’s cormorant, Sharp shinned Hawk, Osprey, Bald eagle, American gold finches, Vaux’s swift, Barn and Violet green swallows, and Rufous humming birds among others.
